Everyone has a goal or goals they strive to achieve sometime throughout life. My personal goal has always been to strive to the top and bring others with me. A rather vague way to describe it I suppose. However, a more descriptive version of stating that goal would be to say that I aim to be successful at shaping my future starting with a career in which benefits the many. Ever since I learned about the mechanics of Sociology and the career paths within it, I knew I had to do everything to hone my skills for a sustainable career. I attend the University of California Riverside (UCR) and my major is Sociology with an emphasis on family and gender. I aim to be a Social Worker. I wish to extend any form of help possible to those in our communities who need it most. Furthermore, an opportunity in this field will involve myself even more so in community engagement. I want to develop a deep understanding of the type of lives our fellow citizens lead. Ranging from different sides of the spectrum to the other.

Additionally, research and internship positions available are extremely beneficial to a prospective applicant as myself. Research is necessary for any objective in mind to contribute to society. An internship opportunity is also very helpful in pursuing such research. To be a productive human being means that I must be able to produce any aid possible to the world. Internships that involve research will give me the resources I need to contribute new information or clarity to the world so that it can advance forward. I want to be one of the steeping stones to an enhancement in people’s lives.

To be a great leader is not a simple task. The qualities a great leader possesses are that of integrity, vision, ambition, creativity, responsibility, and the ability to inspire. Individuals that manifest these traits are often respectable scholars of society. I believe that I exert these qualities. As a part of both the varsity swim team and the National Honors Society(NHS) during my High School career, there lies the opportunity to lead. There is a varsity team and a junior varsity team. As a captain, it is my duty to encourage, help, and inspire the next generation of academic athletes. Competitive swimming is one of the most challenging sports, and when time arrives for the season to start everyone tends to get extremely anxious and doubtful. As a student leader, it is vital for me to raise their spirits, urge them to maintain their health and grades. Also, as a member of the NHS, I must plan an individual service project. Leading a group of my peers, we provided and helped serve food to the local homeless shelter. I aimed to make a small, but positive, impact on my community. Perhaps the action could be another small step for improving our surroundings. Now that I am in university, I am a part of the University Honors program which has further strengthened those skills.
